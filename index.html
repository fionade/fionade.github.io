Research Interests

My work focuses on designing synergistic interaction of humans with intelligent technologies. In particular, I research how laypersons can benefit from AI techniques such as computer vision and context sensing in use cases such as individually relevant and engaging learning experiences. My collaborators and I apply a user-centred design perspective: we develop and implement concepts and evaluate them in empirical studies in the lab and in everyday environments.

Bio

As a researcher and PhD candidate at the LMU media informatics lab, I conducted HCI research, supervised bachelor's and master's theses, and assisted lectures and practical courses (e.g. on web programming, Android development, user experience).

Before starting my PhD, I worked as a researcher at KnowCenter, a centre for applied data-driven research. My professional experience also includes teaching assistance, mobile app development for a start-up company, and freelance web development.

I obtained a bachelor's degree in media informatics from LMU Munich and a master's degree in computer science from TU Graz. In my master's studies, I focused on multimedia information systems and knowledge technologies.

Through my volunteering activities, I have gained experience in team leadership, project management, and the organisation of events. In 2016, I was educated as a soft-skill trainer and, since then, have coordinated and conducted several soft-skill workshops for students.
